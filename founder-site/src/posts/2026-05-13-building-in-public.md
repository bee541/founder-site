---
title: "Building in Public: Why Transparency Matters"
date: "2026-05-13"
excerpt: "Sharing progress, failures, and learnings openly — because the journey matters as much as the destination."
---

Building in public isn't just a trend — it's a philosophy.

When I started building projects, I realized that every failure was a lesson worth sharing. Every small win was compounded by telling someone about it.

So I started posting everything. The messy parts. The "this isn't working" parts. The "I have no idea what I'm doing" parts.

And something unexpected happened: people started following along. Not because they wanted to see a perfect founder journey, but because they wanted to see a real one.

Now, every project I build has a public dimension. Whether it's through Bluesky updates, blog posts, or open commits — the story is there for anyone who wants to read it.

If you're building something, consider sharing it. Not just the wins. The whole story.
