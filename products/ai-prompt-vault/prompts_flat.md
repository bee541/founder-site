# AI Prompt Vault — 73 Production-Ready Prompts


## Social Media & Reels

### Hook-first TikTok caption
> Write a punchy first-line hook for a TikTok about {TOPIC}. Must land in 2 seconds. No preamble. Hook structure: [question OR bold statement OR scenario] + [what it promises] — {MAXLEN} characters max.

### LinkedIn millionaire post
> Write a LinkedIn post from the POV of a 30-year-old operator who quietly runs {N} revenue. No bragging. Direct, slightly dry, specific numbers. Format: 3-4 short paragraphs. End with one question. No hashtags.

### Instagram carousel opener
> Write slide 1 of 8 for an Instagram carousel about {TOPIC}. Hook must interrupt scrolling. Subtitle line must promise a specific outcome. No clichés. Max 30 words total on slide 1.

### YouTube Shorts script
> Write a 40-second YouTube Shorts script on '{TOPIC}'. Structure: [0-8s hook, 8-32s main point with visual cue, 32-40s CTA]. Screen direction notes in brackets []. Total words under 100.

### Twitter/X thread starter
> Write a 7-tweet thread about {TOPIC}. Tweet 1 must be controversial but defensible. Tweets 2-6 each have one insight + one concrete example. Tweet 7 ends hard CTA. No filler.

### Meme caption for Twitter
> Write a Twitter caption that pairs with a industry-insider meme about {TOPIC}. Two sentences. Punchy. Insider knowledge shows through.

### Instagram Stories swipe sequence
> Write 5 slides for an Instagram Stories sequence about {TOPIC}. Each slide is one sentence or phrase, maximum 10 words. Progressive build to a CTA. No hashtags.

### Engagement bait comment reply
> You're a founder who just posted '{TOPIC}' on LinkedIn. Someone commented: 'How would you actually do this in practice?' Write a reply that's genuinely useful, 40-60 words, ends with a question that continues the thread.

### Threadless single-tweet thought leader
> Write one tweet that positions the author as a serious operator in {NICHE}. Must be quotable, specific enough to feel real, opinionated enough to get replies. Max 280 characters.

### Podcast show notes boilerplate
> Write show notes for a 30-min podcast episode titled '{TITLE}'. Include: 3-5 key takeaways as bullets, 1 quote pull, timestamps summary. Format clean markdown. Don't write the full transcript.

### TikTok trending sound caption
> Write a 3-sentence caption for a trending TikTok sound about {TOPIC}. First line must be a hook that references the sound's energy. Second line is the insight. Third line ends with a question.

### Facebook post copy
> Write a Facebook post for {NICHE} about {TOPIC}. Conversational, warm, mobile-first. Short paragraphs. One clear action at the end. No corporate tone.


## Copywriting & Sales

### Cold email opening line
> Write a cold email opening line for reaching {TITLE} at {COMPANY_TYPE}. Must not mention the product. Must signal awareness of their specific situation. Max 25 words.

### Sales landing page hook
> Write the H1 + subhead for a landing page selling {PRODUCT} to {AUDIENCE}. H1 is one sentence, subhead expands. Combined under 15 words. Benefit-first, not feature-first.

### Direct response ad copy
> Write a 150-word Facebook ad for {PRODUCT}. Attention hook → pain → solution → proof → CTA. One benefit per sentence. Make it feel like a human wrote it, not an ad.

### Email subject lines
> Write 5 email subject lines for a newsletter about {TOPIC}. Each targets a different angle: curiosity, pain, urgency, social proof, utility. Label each with its strategy.

### Call-to-action button copy
> Write 15 CTA button labels for a checkout page. Mix urgency, clarity, and value variety. Each 1-6 words. Examples of good vs bad included in output.

### Product launch announcement
> Write a product launch email for {PRODUCT_NAME}. FOMO structure: context → problem → solution → launch details → deadline → P.S. bonus. Subject line + body. Warm, not hype.

### Sales objection response
> Write a response to the objection: '{OBJECTION}' when selling {PRODUCT_TYPE}. Use the Feel-Felt-Found framework. Maximum 80 words. No defensiveness. Empathic + evidence-based.

### Landing page conversion funnel copy
> Write a short-form landing page for {PRODUCT}. Sections: H1, Problem statement (1 para), Solution overview (1 para), 3 key benefits as bullets, Social proof placeholder, Final CTA. Total under 250 words.

### LinkedIn InMail template
> Write a 120-word LinkedIn InMail to a {TITLE} introducing {OFFER}. Must feel like warm outreach, not cold spam. One specific reason you're reaching out to them specifically. Clear, professional CTA.

### Product description for e-commerce
> Write a product description for {PRODUCT_NAME}. Start with the transformation it creates. List 3 features as direct benefits. One social proof line. 1 concise paragraph. No bullet points.

### One-liner elevator pitch
> Write a one-line elevator pitch for {PRODUCT} addressed to {AUDIENCE}. Follow this formula: We help [audience] achieve [desired outcome] by [unique method] — max 25 words.

### Referral program copy
> Write the activation copy for a referral program. One email, 80 words. Explains: the reward for referring, the reward for being referred, how to share the link. Intriguing, not cheesy.


## Email & Communication

### Weekly newsletter intro
> Write a newsletter intro for {TOPIC}. 30-40 words. Warm, specific, teases the newsletter content without revealing everything. Feels like a conversation from a peer.

### Follow-up email (nurture sequence)
> Write a nurture email sent 3 days after a content download about {TOPIC}. Reference the previous touchpoint, add a second perspective, and link to next action. 120 words. Friendly but not salesy.

### Re-engagement email for dormant subscribers
> Write a re-engagement email for subscribers who haven't opened in 90 days. Self-deprecating angle about sending too much content. Offer a free exclusive resource for reopening. 80 words. Conversational.

### Bug/status update email
> Write a status update email for a product incident affecting {FEATURE}. Confident tone. What happened, what you're doing, expected resolution. 90 words. No jargon. One apology line. Sharp transparency.

### Team update announcement
> Write a team update section for a startup all-hands email. Section: '{TOPIC}'. 2 short paragraphs. Specific metrics, specific people called out. Celebrate real progress.

### Interview request subject + body
> Write a podcast interview request email. Subject line + 80-word body. Mention one specific reason you chose this guest. Make the ask specific (interview format, timing, topic). No lengthy pitch deck attachment in the email.

### Cancellation survey email
> Write a polite cancellation confirmation email for {PRODUCT/TYPE}. Thank the customer, explain what they lose, give them one no-fuss way to return. End warm. Don't beg. 60 words.

### Personalized outreach to a lukewarm lead
> Write an email to a lead who downloaded '{ASSET}' but didn't book a call. Reference what they downloaded, connect it to one pain of {NPC}, one genuine question about their situation. No CTA beyond 'reply when you're free.' 100 words.

### Task reminder follow-up tone matching
> You're a project manager reminding a teammate their deliverable is 2 days overdue. Write 3 different versions: Formal workplace, informal Slack, and urgent project-risk. Under 30 words each.

### Customer success check-in at 30 days
> Write a CSM check-in email for a customer on day 30 of using {PRODUCT/TYPE}. Ask 3 specific discovery questions, not 'how is everything?'. Tone: advisory ally, not account manager. 100 words.


## SEO & Content

### SEO meta description
> Write an SEO meta description for a blog post about {TOPIC} targeting '{KEYWORDphrase}'. Max 160 characters. Include keyword naturally. CTA at the end: 'Learn More'.

### Blog post outline
> Write a 6-section outline for a 1500-word blog post on '{TITLE}'. Each section has: (H2) heading, 2-3 sub-bullets, and one key insight to deliver. Include intro and conclusion outlines. Target SEO vs real value split: 60/40.

### Listicle format intro
> Write the intro for a listicle blog post: '{TOPIC}'. Name the count in the title. First paragraph creates the problem. Second teases the solution. Hook in first sentence. 60-80 words. Perfect for SEO.

### Alt text for images
> Write descriptive alt text for 3 images in a blog post about {TOPIC}: (1) hero product shot, (2) before/after shot, (3) process infographic. Each alt text under 100 characters. Include relevant keyword naturally.

### H1 + opening paragraph
> Write an H1 and 80-word opening paragraph for a landing page about {PRODUCT} targeting '{KEYWORDPHRASE}.' H1 splits benefit from audience. First paragraph establishes credibility + starts the story.

### YouTube video description with chapters
> Write a YouTube video description for a 15-min video about {TOPIC}. Include: 2-paragraph hook, chapter timestamps (5 chapters), top 3 takeaways, call to subscribe. 250 words max for main description.

### FAQ section for product page
> Write 5 FAQ entries for a product page about {PRODUCT}. Mixes pre-purchase questions, common objections, and post-purchase logistics. Format: Question (bold) + Answer (40-60 words). FAQ section header included.

### Resource page intro
> Write an intro for a resource page that lists 25+ free tools for {NICHE} operators. Compelling opening: admit a pain every reader has felt. Name the value: 'curated, not just listed'. 60 words.

### Pillar page content brief
> Write a content brief for a pillar article: '{TOPIC}.' Include: primary keyword, secondary keywords, word count target, word count per section, internal link strategy, sources to cite, and E-E-A-T signal notes. Format as structured brief.

### Competitor comparison intro
> Write the intro section for a '{PRODUCT_A} vs {PRODUCT_B}' comparison article. POV: experienced user of both. Hook: 'if you've used both, you know exactly where this is going.' Fair tone. 80 words. Lead without judgment.

### Guest post pitch email
> Write an email pitch to a blogger in the {NICHE} niche offering a guest post on '{TOPIC}'. Include: one genuine compliment on their recent content, the proposal angle, and why your readers overlap with theirs. Show familiarity; don't outsource the compliment. 120 words.


## Code & Technical Writing

### API documentation structure
> Write the endpoint documentation template for a REST API endpoint: GET /api/{RESOURCE}. Include: description, request params table, response JSON schema, and one example request + response. Structured markdown.

### Code review feedback
> Write feedback on a PR that changes {COMPONENT} using {TECHNOLOGY}. Highlight 2 strengths, suggest 2 improvements (one performance, one readability), and close with encouragement. 80 words max. Specific technical details.

### Git commit message
> Write a conventional commit message for a change that {CHANGE}. Type is {feat|fix|refactor|docs}. Scope: {SCOPE}. Max 50 characters subject line, body explains what and why (not how) under 72 chars per line.

### README template
> Write the structure for a new developer tool's README. Sections: Badges → What it does → Installation → Quick start → Configuration → Usage examples → Contributing → License. Each section with placeholder content. Format as Markdown.

### API error message
> Write an API error message for a failed authentication attempt. Friendly but technical. Include: error code, what went wrong, what to do next, and where to find the auth docs. 2 sentences. Not a stack trace.

### Debugging log analysis prompt
> Write a prompt to ask an AI assistant to analyze a bug in {TECHNOLOGY}. Request format: root cause (plain language), code location (file + line), reproduction steps, and suggested fix. Include example error log snippet.

### Terminal instruction for a dev
> Write a terminal instruction to run a build script in {TECHNOLOGY}. Include: the full command, expected output, and a 1-line check for success. Friendly but authoritative. One paragraph.

### OpenAPI spec snippet
> Write an OpenAPI 3.0 snippet for a POST endpoint: '{ENDPOINT}'. Include: path, description, request body (one field), response (201 status), security. Output valid YAML. 20 lines max.

### Stack Overflow question
> Write a Stack Overflow question about {TECHNOLOGY}. Title must be specific and searchable. Body includes: what you're doing, what you've tried, minimal reproducible example, tags. Targeted at getting a real answer from someone who's solved this.

### SQL query explanation
> Write a plain-language explanation of what this SQL query does: '{QUERY}'. Break it down by clause. Explain the business logic it's solving. For a non-technical ops stakeholder. 120 words max.

### DALL-E brand illustration
> Write a precise DALL-E prompt for a {STYLE} illustration of {SUBJECT} for a {INDUSTRY} brand. Style reference: {STYLE_REFERENCE}. Key visual must be recognizable at small size (social card thumbnail). Text output is 'no text' aside from the brand element.

### YouTube thumbnail prompt

### Product photography concept
> Write a Midjourney concept prompt for product photography of {PRODUCT}. Studio lighting. White or {THEME} background. Centered subject. Professional catalogue_photo quality. --ar 3:2 --style raw. Followed by parameters only.

### Website header hero image

### Social card thumbnail

### Infographic concept prompt

### Instagram carousel background

## Personal Branding & Bio

### Twitter/X bio formula
> Write a Twitter bio for {ROLE} who {SPECIFIC_THING}. 150 char max. One credential marker, one personality line, one CTA. Format as single line. No quotes.

### LinkedIn headline
> Write a LinkedIn headline for {ROLE} at {COMPANY}. 220 char max. Format: [Role] | [What they've done] | [What they're building next]. Specific numbers. Not generic.

### Instagram bio
> Write an Instagram bio for {PERSONA}. Covers: 1-line bio, 3 topics pinned in the bio link, and the bio link description. Warm, human. Under 150 words.

### Personal brand tagline
> Write a personal brand tagline (40-60 chars) for someone who {SPECIFIC_ACHIEVEMENT}. Feels earned, not claimed. Avoids buzzwords. One sentence.

### Grand-slam bio for a conference profile
> Write a conference speaker bio for {NAME} who does {SPECIALTY}. Third person. One specific achievement, one current focus, one sentence about what they'll talk about. Exactly 75 words.

### Course instructor bio
> Write an instructor bio for a course page teaching '{TOPIC}' by {PERSONA}. Credentials (specific), teaching philosophy in one sentence, one personal detail to humanise, 150 words. Feels deserved.


## Quick Decision & Action

### Decision matrix
> You need to choose between {OPTION_A} and {OPTION_B} for {DECISION}. Write a weighted decision matrix with: 4 criteria (weighted), score each option out of 10 per criterion, final recommendation with one-line reasoning. 150 words max.

### Five whys root cause
> A team at {COMPANY_NICHE} is experiencing '{PROBLEM}'. Run the Five Whys. Actual answers, not generic. End with one specific action the team must take. 120 words. No jargon.

### Heckler call-out response
> You're on a LinkedIn post talking about '{TOPIC}' and someone says 'That only works if you're lucky / have connections / etc.' Write a confident, generous reply that doesn't take the bait. Data-backed. 60-80 words. Wins the thread.

### Quick win list
> Write a list of 5 quick wins a {ROLE} at a {STAGE} startup can implement this week related to {TOPIC}. Each is one sentence. Each costs no money. Each takes under 2 hours. Total list is 8 lines.
